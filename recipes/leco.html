Slovak Lečo

Description

Lečo is a traditional Slovak dish that’s warm, comforting, and bursting with flavor. It’s made primarily from bell peppers, tomatoes, and onions, which simmer together to create a rich and savory vegetable stew. The dish is often seasoned with paprika, giving it a subtle, smoky depth that complements the sweetness of the peppers and the acidity of the tomatoes.

Lečo can be enjoyed on its own as a vegetarian dish, or it can be made heartier with the addition of smoked sausage and eggs, turning it into a filling meal. This simple yet satisfying recipe is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner and pairs beautifully with fresh bread or rice to soak up all the delicious flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or olive oil)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 bell peppers (red, yellow, or green), sliced into strips
  • 4-5 medium tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika (sweet or smoked)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 sausages (optional), sliced
  • 4 eggs (optional)
  • Fresh parsley or chives for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook for about 5 minutes, until they turn soft and golden.
  2. Add the garlic and bell peppers to the pan. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until the peppers start to soften.
  3. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and paprika. Season with salt and pepper, then reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 15-20 minutes, until the vegetables are soft and well combined.
  4. If desired, add the sausage slices to the pan, stirring them into the vegetable mixture. Cook until the sausage is heated through.
  5. For a heartier dish, crack the eggs directly into the pan and stir gently to scramble them into the lečo mixture. Continue cooking until the eggs are fully set.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley or chives and serve hot with crusty bread, rice, or potatoes.
