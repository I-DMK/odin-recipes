Norwegian Fårikål

Description

Fårikål is a traditional Norwegian stew made with lamb, cabbage, and whole black peppercorns. This hearty dish is especially popular in the autumn and is considered Norway’s national dish. It’s simple yet flavorful, with a slow-cooked texture that brings out the rich taste of the lamb and the sweetness of the cabbage.

This recipe is perfect for those who enjoy classic, slow-cooked meals that require minimal ingredients. Fårikål is best served hot and is traditionally accompanied by boiled potatoes, making it a comforting meal for chilly weather.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900g) lamb shoulder or neck, cut into chunks
  • 1 large head of cabbage, cut into wedges
  • 2-3 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2-3 cups water
  • Boiled potatoes, for serving

Steps

  1. In a large pot, layer the lamb pieces at the bottom. Sprinkle with a portion of the salt and peppercorns.
  2. Add a layer of cabbage wedges on top of the lamb. Repeat the layering of lamb, salt, peppercorns, and cabbage until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of cabbage.
  3. Add enough water to the pot to almost cover the ingredients, usually about 2-3 cups.
  4. Bring the pot to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let the stew simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the lamb is tender and the cabbage is soft.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning with additional salt if needed.
  6. Serve the fårikål hot, accompanied by boiled potatoes. Enjoy!
